Week 8
The point about whether artists are effective describers of their own work raises important questions about the subjective nature of artistic interpretation. Indeed, artists often view their creations through the lens of their intent, which can sometimes diverge from the broader impact on audiences. While artists can offer a unique and valuable perspective in the description process, there's merit in involving collaborators or editors who can provide an alternative viewpoint.
Week 9
In the realm of hypermodernism, symbols gain distinct recognition, often drawing from the digital and cultural fragments to carry esoteric meaning for the initiated. This collection, centered around Disney's icons and typography, shows how these references have taken root in certain graphic design circles. By dissecting this collection as a form of textual analysis, we can uncover deeper layers of significance. This evolution in hypermodernism aligns with the emergence of animation during the modern period, with Walt Disney's 1923 inception coinciding with the Weimar Bauhaus's shift towards commercial production. This showcases how early animation and modern art shared a common exploration of flat forms and aesthetics.
Week 10
The experience of designing the project, a non-English programming language using Arabic, highlights the deep-rooted linguistic biases in the world of technology and programming. While it offers an alternative for non-English speakers, it also exposes practical challenges, such as platform limitations and compatibility issues. It is so important to consider linguistic diversity and inclusivity in technology to create a more equitable and accessible tech ecosystem.
